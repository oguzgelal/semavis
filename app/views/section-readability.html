Readability score indicates how easy your text is to read. A text hard to read doesn't necessarily mean you have a bad text. But the more readable your text is, the larger audience it will attract. Depending on your needs, if you are writing your thesis you could keep this bar close to hard to read, as your intended audience is most likely to read and understand your text. However if you are writing an article for the general public to read, you might want to get this score closer to "easy to read".

Here are the simple steps you should take to improve your readability score:

  • Use shorter sentences.
  • Reduce jargon, acronyms, and “insider” words.
  • Use an active voice.
  • Turn complex points into simple maxims.
  • Stay on point.
  • Use an application that analyses your text in terms of readability.
